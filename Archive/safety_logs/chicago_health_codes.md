# Chicago Food Safety & Health Code Reference

This document summarizes key sections of the Chicago Municipal Code (Title 7, Chapter 7-38) and the Illinois Food Service Sanitation Code (77 Ill. Adm. Code 750) relevant to allergen management and cross-contact prevention.

## 1. Allergen Awareness Training (IL Senate Bill 0667 / 2026 Update)
* **Requirement:** All certified food service sanitation managers must undergo additional training specifically in allergen awareness.
* **Key Protocol:** Restaurants are required to have a clear procedure for when a customer notifies staff of a food allergy.
* **AI Logic Point:** If a staff response indicates a lack of knowledge about allergens, the system should flag it as a "High Risk" protocol failure under Chicago health standards.

## 2. Cross-Contact Prevention (Section 7-38-010)
* **Equipment Safety:** Utensils and food-contact surfaces must be cleaned and sanitized between uses to prevent cross-contamination.
* **Shared Surfaces:** Special attention is required for shared equipment like deep fryers, grills, and cutting boards.
* **Hidden Risk:** Chicago code allows for "shared use" of certain equipment if sanitized, but for anaphylactic users, "sanitized" may not be enough to remove protein traces.

## 3. Labeling and "Truth in Menu" (Section 7-38-015)
* **Accurate Descriptions:** It is a violation of Chicago Health Code to misrepresent ingredients. 
* **House Specialties:** "Secret ingredients" or "House Sauces" must be fully disclosed to the Health Department and to any customer declaring an allergy.
* **AI Logic Point:** If a menu uses vague terms like "Chef's Blend" without detailing ingredients, the system should generate a "Mandatory Inquiry" for the user.

## 4. Cold and Hot Storage (Section 7-38-010)
* **Storage Order:** Raw proteins (fish/shellfish) must be stored below or separate from ready-to-eat foods to prevent drip-contamination.
* **AI Logic Point:** In buffet or deli settings, the risk of "drip" or "spoon-swapping" is noted as a primary cause of accidental exposure in high-density Chicago eateries.

## 5. Staff Communication Requirements
* **Standard of Care:** The Person in Charge (PIC) must be able to demonstrate knowledge of the "Big 9" allergens.
* **System Prompt:** "When analyzing staff responses, check for keywords like 'manager checked' or 'chef confirmed' which align with Chicago's PIC (Person in Charge) requirements for higher safety confidence."

## 6. Chicago Health Inspection Focus Areas
* **Violation 25:** Proper cooling time and temperature.
* **Violation 28:** Toxic substances properly identified/stored.
* **Violation 43:** In-use utensils: properly stored (shared water wells for ice cream scoops are a major cross-contact risk noted in Chicago inspections).
