# Hidden Allergen & Cross-Contact Reference Guide

This document provides a comprehensive list of hidden names, technical terms, and high-risk processing methods for major food allergens. This data is intended for use by AI reasoning agents to identify non-obvious risks in restaurant menus and ingredient lists.

## 1. Dairy (Milk)
**Hidden Names & Derivatives:**
* Casein, Caseinate (Ammonium, Calcium, Magnesium, Potassium, Sodium)
* Whey (Delactosed, Demineralized, Protein Concentrate)
* Lactalbumin, Lactoferrin, Lactoglobulin
* Ghee, Anhydrous Milk Fat
* Recaldent (found in some chewing gums)
* Tagatose (sweetener derived from dairy)
* Curds, Rennet Casein
* Nougat (often contains milk)

**High-Risk Processing Contexts:**
* Baked goods brushed with "wash" (milk/egg).
* Non-dairy creamers (often contain Sodium Caseinate).
* Caramel and Butterscotch candies.

## 2. Peanuts
**Hidden Names & Derivatives:**
* Arachis oil (another name for peanut oil)
* Goober peas
* Mandelonas (peanuts soaked in almond flavoring)
* Hydrolyzed plant/vegetable protein (can be peanut-sourced)
* Lupin (a legume that cross-reacts heavily with peanut allergies)

**High-Risk Processing Contexts:**
* Shared fryers (especially in Asian or fast-food environments).
* Cuisines: Thai, Vietnamese, African, and Mexican (mole sauces).
* "Natural Flavors" in candy or snacks.

## 3. Wheat & Gluten
**Hidden Names & Derivatives:**
* Seitan (wheat gluten)
* Bulgur, Farina, Semolina, Spelt, Farro, Einkorn
* Triticale
* Soy Sauce (traditionally made with wheat)
* Malt (Malt Vinegar, Malt Extract, Malt Syrup)
* Atta, Maida (Indian wheat flours)
* Hydrolyzed wheat protein

**High-Risk Processing Contexts:**
* Shared pasta boiling water.
* "Dusting" of flour on surfaces or for thickening sauces (roux).
* Imitation crab (Surimi) often uses wheat starch as a binder.

## 4. Egg
**Hidden Names & Derivatives:**
* Albumin/Ovalbumin
* Lysozyme (used as a preservative)
* Lecithin (can be egg-sourced, though often soy)
* Vitellin
* Meringue, Pavlova
* Surimi

**High-Risk Processing Contexts:**
* Shiny glazes on bread or pastries.
* Custards, aioli, and many "house" salad dressings.
* Fresh pasta (traditionally contains egg).

## 5. Soy
**Hidden Names & Derivatives:**
* Edamame
* Miso, Natto, Tempeh
* Tamari, Teriyaki sauce
* Tocopherols (can be soy-sourced Vitamin E)
* Vegetable gum / Vegetable broth (often contains soy)

**High-Risk Processing Contexts:**
* Vegetable oils (often a blend including soy).
* Plant-based meat substitutes.

## 6. Shellfish & Fish
**Hidden Names & Derivatives:**
* Glucosamine (often derived from shellfish)
* Worcestershire sauce (contains anchovies)
* Caesar dressing (contains anchovies)
* Nuoc Mam (Fish sauce)
* Belacan (Shrimp paste)
* Surimi

**High-Risk Processing Contexts:**
* Steam or vapors from boiling pots (airborne risk).
* Shared grills for meat and seafood.
